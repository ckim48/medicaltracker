Project Overview

Discover our specially tailored mobile application designed to revolutionize sleep monitoring. Utilizing advanced sensors in mobile devices, our app collects and analyzes sleep data, alerting supervisors of health-threatening issues.

Objective

Initially designed to cater to elderly individuals living independently with disruptive sleep disorders, this application gathers, analyzes, and visually represents sleep data. The aim is to promptly identify abnormal sleeping movements or behaviors in real-time, for ultimately enhancing intervention strategies.

App Gallery

The landing page features a “Tip of the Day” and a “Get Started” button for tracking sleep data and improving sleep quality.

For users interested in in-depth analysis, detailed sound and movement data is available. The black line represents current data, and the red line indicates the threshold..

Users can view their recorded sound and movement data on the “History page”. The history data is shown in chronological order and shows data stored during one session.

The interface for recording sleep data also serves as a dark screen to avoid disturbing sleeping users. Notifications are sent to the caretaker if the sleeper surpasses predefined movement or noise thresholds.

Pressing the “Start” button on the main page takes you to a page with current time and sleep tracking instructions.

The “Caretaker” page enables users to select one caretaker to attend to them, with the flexibility for each caretaker to oversee multiple patients.

The “Admin Page” is used to check the status of patients. It shows the number of patients and the patient status. You can select a patient to see their more detailed information.

Next Steps

The pilot version is scheduled to start in December of 2023, with the preliminary testing phase spanning from the end of October to December 2023, involving 12 carefully selected participants. After successfully completing the enhanced user testing phase, the application is expected to broaden its user scope to local nursing homes.
